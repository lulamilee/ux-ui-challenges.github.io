The Scene Cinema App

UI/UX Design Case Study
Client
bOnline
Sector
Digital Booking
Challenge
The current manual ticket booking process means that customers have to interact with a staff member to purchase tickets. The manual process currently only allows purchasing movie tickets on the day with no pre-booking. The staff member uses a sheet of paper with the cinema seating arrangement to show the customer which seats are available and crosses out a seat after payment is taken.
My Role
Research, Wireframing, Interface Design

How I Started

Since this is a solo project I was looking for everyday problems and challenges that people face on a daily basis. One day when I was at the cinema I realised how frustrating it can be waiting in line to purchase a movie ticket, especially since the ticket machines were having issues. I began to think about the everyday customer who just wants to arrive get their ticket, pick a seat, their food and go into the screen showing. I imagined the frustrations they would face waiting in line for people, who were indecisive. I thought "how would I correct this inconvenience?"

What is better

I created an app that provides you with the opportunity to not only book your movie, seating and food in advance, but also gives you the nearest cinema in your area?

Problems, Goals And Solutions To Solve

The primary goal of The Scene Cinema App?

- Create an app where we people can sign up and book movie tickets online.

What problems do I want to solve here?

  • Waiting line is time consuming, nobody likes it.
  • Indecisive people can be an annoyance to other customers.
  • Sometimes the ticket machines have maintenance issues.
  • Not enough staff for customers to be served in a timely manner.
  • Rather eat before the movie starts.

The Solution

Create an App where people can book movie tickets, view listings and order food. The App will be called Scene Cinema. It should be easy to use and intuitive. If it seems to slow, clumsy and or complicated this defeats the purpose.

Competitors and User Requirements

My competitors

I started by researching competitors to see what they're doing. In this case it was Cinema, Sterinkor and NuMetro. The things I'm looking for here, is what I like and dislike about their app flow/content and how I can incorporate what they do and possibly make The Scene Cinema App much better than my competitors.

User Research

To understand the users better, I have to think from their point of view and keep the content overwhelming but avoid distractions which often is the reason people back out. The experiences and challenges faced by users are: their behaviour/their focus during interacting, payment process, booking experience, usability and ease to understand.

User Stories

In order to insure success I created some requirements that the Scene Cinema App must fulfill in order create a happy customer and convergence:

  • As a user I should be able to easily book a ticket/s online.
  • I should see a list of movies and trailers that are available to watch.
  • I should be able to see the food menu and what size and quantity to order.
  • As a repeat customer, I should have the option to select my favorite meal.
  • As a user I should be able to book the seats that are available.
  • As a user I should be able to select what cinema location to see the movie in.
  • As a user I should be able to create an account to and save food order history.
  • See currently showing movies and be able to login/sign up.
  • Have the option to book a time on when to have my food ready. I may not want to eat during the movie.
  • I should be able to pay using Paypal or other payment gateways options

Mood Board And Sketching

Mood Board

I usually start with a mood board before any sketching. It consists of various websites and apps that I'll collect from my competitors, Pinterest, Dribbble and Google Images The purpose of this is just to get some ideas on what fonts to use, colours, layout ideas, interactivity and overall mood. This is similar to me researching about competitors.

Sketching

‍After collecting a sufficient amount of inspiration from the mood board, I then go into the low-fidelity sketching phase. This helped me get some ideas down, what ideas to keep and improve upon. I looked into the method of how my competitors go about booking a movie and then I incorporate it into my sketches for better approach, mix & matching whatever ideas I get from the Mood Board. It's important to note that I always have the User Stories handy with me. This helps me to figure out if what I'm sketching, falls inline with those requirements.

Wireframes

After being satisfied with the low-fidelity sketches, I created mid-fidelity for a more accurate representation of the final design. The tool I used is called Balsamiq Mockups. This is useful because it helped me to decide to move forward or go back to low-fidelity again. The main page for example, I wanted the user to see what movies was the most popular and then be able to go to the movie listings, rather than have them register first

More on Wireframes

This is all the wireframes created. During the process I add in some indications of interactivity of what button does what and where it leads to.
Prototyping Phase In order to test if the flow works, Balsamiq has a simple prototyping function that connects you to each wireframe by linking a specific button to the next desired page. Based on the User Stories it seemed to work quite well.

Colors

Choosing colours is key in design, because they can significantly affect the overall design positively or negatively. Psychologically colours have an emotional impact on us and as mentioned before, I wanted something cool and visual, I didn't want to go with blue as a default choice. Using my Mood Board I looked for the colours that I don't see all the time. One of the designs I saw had utilised purple. It's known to not only be associated with royalty, but also can be wise, creative and imaginative. That's why I chose it as a primary colour, I believed it would be a nice compliment to the cool and visual feel I was going for.

Colour Palette

I used the website, Coolors.co to experiment with different shades of my primary and other supporting colours. Purple is pretty dark and I wasn't going for a dark themed app, so I went for a lighter shade (#734b6d) and used that to create a subtle gradient. Also coupling that with a white background was going the keep the visual aspect alive.

UI Design For The Scene Cinema App

Once I tested out all usability flow, I started designing the final screens in Figma.

Layout

Previously I mentioned that I wanted to couple the subtle gradient with a white background as I wasn't going for a dark themed app. I got this idea to do a wavy background with faded image, from one of the Mood Board designs. It had a sharp angular shape with an image, so I experimented and made it work. It was tricky however since there were some info which didn't look readable on the dark gradient background. However a subtle drop shadow fixed that.

I learned how important it is to really try to understand design from a users perspective, otherwise you design for all the wrong reasons. This essentially leads to a waste of time, an unfulfilling product and an unhappy customer, which is one of my fears.
